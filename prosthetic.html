Mechanically Enhanced Prosthetic Foot

University of Waterloo · 2023

This project explores the design and mechanical validation of a simplified prosthetic foot model. Prosthetics play a vital role in restoring or enhancing human mobility, and understanding the mechanical properties of their materials is essential to ensure functionality and durability. Using PLA filament and tensile testing results, a custom material profile was created in SolidWorks. Finite Element Analysis (FEA) simulated realistic loading—specifically a 190 N force at the ankle—and results were validated against hand calculations.

Key Findings

Material Properties of PLA:

  • Young’s Modulus: 463.37 MPa
  • Yield Stress: 16.8 MPa
  • Ultimate Stress: 28.89 MPa
  • Density: 1.25 g/cm³

Validation of Simulation:

  • Hand-calculated displacement: 0.281 m
  • SolidWorks displacement: 0.0117 m
  • Difference due to simplified hand model assumptions.

Design Improvements:

  • I-beam cross-section increased moment of inertia.
  • Widened and filleted ankle base for better load distribution.
  • Final volume: 11,800.37 mm³
