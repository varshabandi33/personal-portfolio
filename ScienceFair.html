Bacteria and Microorganisms: Humans’ Ally Against Cancer

Silver Medal – Regional Science Fair · 2021

This project was a novel research proposal and literature review that explored the potential of bacteria and microorganisms in modern cancer therapies. Rather than relying on traditional treatments like chemotherapy and radiation, which often damage healthy cells, our investigation focused on using genetically engineered bacteria to target and destroy tumors with greater precision and fewer side effects.

The study reviewed emerging scientific studies, models, and therapeutic strategies that leverage bacterial selectivity, synthetic genetic circuits, and immunotherapy integration. The project was recognized with a Silver Medal at the Regional Science Fair for its innovation and depth of research.

Key Factors

Tumor-Targeting Bacterial Strains:

  • Used genetically modified S. Typhimurium and E. coli models
  • Engineered for hypoxic tumor environments and high motility
  • Enabled selective proliferation in tumor tissue over normal cells

Artificial Genetic Circuits:

  • Enabled drug delivery, self-destruction, and immune activation
  • Featured controlled gene expression systems and triggers
  • Promoted safety via virulence attenuation and containment

Immunotherapy Applications:

  • Delivered checkpoint inhibitors (PD-L1, CTLA-4 nanobodies) intratumorally
  • Stimulated localized T-cell immune response
  • Enabled regression of both treated and distant tumors (abscopal effect)

Theranostic Approaches:

  • Integrated tumor detection and treatment using bacteriobots
  • Focused on magnetic navigation and targeted micro-drug release
  • Proposed future use of biosensors and smart diagnostics

Literature-Driven Methodology:

  • Compiled studies from Nature, Science, and medical journals
  • Highlighted engineering principles behind microbial therapeutics
  • Presented as a proposal with cited references and theoretical models
